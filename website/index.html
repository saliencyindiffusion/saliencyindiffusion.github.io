Saliency in Diffusion - Interactive Results

Anonymous authors
CVPR 2024 Submission - Paper ID: 14028

Abstract

We present the first controllable and interpretable saliency prediction approach. This is achieved by incorporating saliency in a diffusion model. Specifically, we leverage a shared latent feature space between the image and saliency modalities to simultaneously generate image edits and predict saliency. We utilize a saliency-guided cross-attention mechanism within the shared latent feature space that performs targeted edits on photometric properties such as contrast, brightness, and color. These edits improve saliency within the desired edited image regions, making our model controllable in the context of saliency Our approach also allows a qualitative interpretation of the photometric properties on which the model bases its predictions. As shown by a user study, our method is able to predict the human visual attention patterns in the generated image edits. We demonstrate the performance of our approach on several saliency benchmarks.
